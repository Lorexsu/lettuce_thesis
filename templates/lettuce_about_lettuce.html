Why Lettuce in Hydroponics?

Lettuce is one of the most popular and successful crops for hydroponic cultivation. Its fast growth cycle, minimal space requirements, and high market demand make it an ideal choice for both commercial and research purposes.

In controlled hydroponic environments, lettuce can be grown year-round with consistent quality, reduced water usage, and minimal pesticide requirements. This makes it perfect for urban farming and sustainable food production systems.

Estrosa is a popular leafy lettuce variety with elongated, frilly leaves and a crisp texture. Its vibrant green color and loose-leaf structure make it ideal for continuous harvest systems. The leaves grow outward from a central core, allowing for easy picking.
Crispy Texture Heat Tolerant High Yield Disease Resistant

Growth Characteristics

  • Forms an open, upright rosette of leaves
  • Excellent regrowth after harvesting outer leaves
  • Maintains quality over extended harvest period
  • Adapts well to various hydroponic systems

Nutritional Benefits

  • Rich in vitamins A, C, and K
  • Good source of folate and iron
  • Contains antioxidants and fiber
  • Low calorie, nutrient-dense option

Best Practices

  • Maintain EC levels between 1.2-1.8 mS/cm
  • Keep pH between 5.5-6.5 for optimal growth
  • Provide 12-16 hours of light daily
  • Ensure good air circulation to prevent disease

Growth Characteristics

  • Forms tight, compact heads with excellent uniformity
  • Smooth, buttery leaves with minimal bitterness
  • Maintains stable color throughout growth cycle
  • Excellent shelf life after harvest

Nutritional Benefits

  • High in vitamins A and C
  • Good source of dietary fiber
  • Contains beneficial minerals like iron and calcium
  • Low in sodium, high in potassium

Best Practices

  • Maintain EC levels between 1.0-1.6 mS/cm
  • Keep pH between 5.8-6.2 for best results
  • Provide consistent 14-16 hours of light
  • Monitor for tip burn in high-growth conditions
